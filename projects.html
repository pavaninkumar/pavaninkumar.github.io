Simulation of Irradiation Effects on Silicon Carbide

Guide:

Prof. Narasimhan Swaminathan, Dept. of Mechanical Engineering, IIT Madras (Jan'14 - present)

Abstract

Objective is to run molecular dynamic simulations to determine the effects of nuclear radiation on silicon carbide structures. Silicon carbide is being considered to replace Zircaloy for the claddings in a nuclear reactor. Claddings are used to enclose nuclear fuel and are hence exposed to high energy neutron radiation. To mimic the effects of neutron radiation, concentrated vacancies were simulated and the effect on the tensile strength studied. Concentrated interstitials and anti-sites shall also be introduced in the future. The simulations were run using LAMMPS MD simulator, along with OVITO, for lattice visualisation, and Scilab for post-processing the results.
